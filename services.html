Physical Therapy

Physical Therapists are licensed professionals who work with people who have sustained disabilities or impairments or limitations in their overall physical function. This can be a result of disease, injury or pathological process. Physical Therapists examine, evaluate, diagnose and develop treatment plans for each patient, through the use of exercise, mobilization, manipulation, heat, cold and elec stimulation.

Cryotherapy

Cryotherapy(ice therapy) is the latest phenomenon used by athletes for last the two decades. Recent studies have suggested that cold water immersion can significantly increase the prevention and rehabilitation of sports injuries. It involves the patient being partially immersed(to waist level) in cold water for 5-10 minutes and for some upper torso injuries immersing(to shoulder level) for 1-2 min. The water temperature can range from 1-14 degrees and contains salts and water jets to penetrate the affected area.

Acupuncture

Dry needling acupuncture is an efficient technique for the treatment of myofascial pain and dysfunction. The approach is based on western anatomical and neurophysiology principles. It concentrates on myofascial trigger points, these are areas which are painful on compression and which give rise to pain, tenderness or motor dysfunction. This treatment is used alongside osteopaths.

Alter G Treadmill

The treadmill works by reducing gravity’s impact by selecting any weight between 20% and 100% of your body weight by 1% increments, improves mobility, strength and safety for patients. It provide a safe way to lose weight and exercise more intensively while unweighted allowing yout to train without pain and reduce stress to joints and muscles and recover effectively and without pain after training and competitions.
